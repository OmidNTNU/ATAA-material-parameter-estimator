Strain Energy Function (SEF)

The aortic wall tissue is assumed to be an incompressible and elastic material reinforced with collagen fibers. To replicate the experimental data, the strain-energy function $\Psi$ is decomposed into an isotropic part $\Psi_{\text{iso}}$ and an anisotropic part $\Psi_{{\rm f}_i}$, $i = 4,6$, representing the matrix contribution and the two families of collagen fibers, respectively. Thus,

$$ \Psi = \Psi_{\text{iso}}(\mathbf{C}) + \sum_{i=4,6} \Psi_{{\rm f}_i}(\mathbf{C}, \mathbf{H}_i) $$

where $\mathbf{C}=\mathbf{F}^{\rm T} \mathbf{F}$ is the right Cauchy-Green tensor. The contributions are specified as:

$$ \Psi_{\text{iso}} = \frac{\mu}{2} \left( {I}_1 - 3 \right) $$ $$ \sum_{i=4,6} \Psi_{{\rm f}_i} = \frac{k_1}{2 k_2} \{\exp [ k_2 \left( E_4 \right)^2] - 1\} + \frac{k_1}{2 k_2} \{\exp[ k_2 \left( E_6 \right)^2] - 1 \} $$

with $E_i = \mathbf{H}_i : (\mathbf{C} - \mathbf{I})$ and where $I_1 = {\rm tr}\mathbf{C}$. The material parameters are $\mu$, $k_1$, and $k_2$. Further terms involve fiber orientation, dispersion ($\kappa_{\rm op}$, $\kappa_{\rm ip}$), and fiber angles ($\alpha_1$, $\alpha_2$).
