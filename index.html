Parameter Estimator for Ascending Thoracic Aortic Wall in Health and Aneurysm

This tool provides constitutive material parameters for the human ascending thoracic aortic wall, suitable for computational modeling. It can be used for both healthy and aneurysmal tissue characterization.

The underlying prediction is powered by a k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) machine learning model. Based on the clinical inputs you provide, the model identifies the most similar patient from a pre-existing dataset and returns the corresponding, experimentally-derived material parameters.

Strain Energy Function

The aortic wall is modeled as a hyperelastic material with an isotropic matrix and two families of collagen fibers. The total strain energy function $\Psi$ is given by:

\[ \Psi = \underbrace{\frac{\textcolor{red}{\mu}}{2} \left( \bar{I}_1 - 3 \right)}_{\text{neo-Hookean}} + \underbrace{\frac{\textcolor{blue}{k_{1}}}{2\textcolor{blue}{k_{2}}} \left( \exp \left[ \textcolor{blue}{k_{2}} \left( \textcolor{green}{E_4} \right)^2 \right] - 1 \right)}_{\text{First fiber family}} + \underbrace{\frac{ \textcolor{blue}{k_{1}}}{2\textcolor{blue}{k_{2}}} \left( \exp \left[ \textcolor{blue}{k_{2}} \left( \textcolor{green}{E_6} \right)^2 \right] - 1 \right)}_{\text{Second fiber family}} \]

The terms $\textcolor{green}{E_4}$ and $\textcolor{green}{E_6}$ are strain-like quantities for each fiber family, which depend on the strain invariants ($I_1, I_4, I_6$) and the structural tensors ($\mathbf{H}_i$):

\[ \textcolor{green}{E_i} = \mathbf{H}_i : (\mathbf{C} - \mathbf{I}) = A I_1 + B I_i + (1 - 3A - B) I_n - 1, \quad i = 4,6 \]

The fiber orientation is defined by two angles, $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$. These represent the mean direction of the two fiber families, measured from the circumferential direction of the aorta, and range from $-90^\circ$ to $+90^\circ$.

The fiber dispersion (how spread out the fibers are) is modeled by an out-of-plane parameter, $\kappa_{\text{op}}$, and an in-plane parameter, $\kappa_{\text{ip}}$. These define the terms $A$ and $B$ as follows:

\[ A = 2 \kappa_{\text{op}} \kappa_{\text{ip}} \quad \text{and} \quad B = 2 \kappa_{\text{op}} (1 - 2 \kappa_{\text{ip}}) \]
