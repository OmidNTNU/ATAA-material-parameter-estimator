Parameter Estimator for Ascending Thoracic Aortic Wall in Health and Aneurysm

This tool provides constitutive material parameters for the human ascending thoracic aortic wall, suitable for computational modeling. It can be used for both healthy and aneurysmal tissue characterization.

The underlying prediction is powered by a k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) machine learning model. Based on the clinical inputs you provide, the model identifies the most similar patient from a pre-existing dataset and returns the corresponding, experimentally-derived material parameters.

Strain Energy Function (SEF)

The aortic wall is modeled as a hyperelastic material with two families of collagen fibers. The strain-energy function $\Psi$ is decomposed into an isotropic part ($\Psi_{\text{iso}}$) and an anisotropic part ($\Psi_{\text{f}}$):

$$ \Psi = \Psi_{\text{iso}}(\mathbf{C}) + \sum_{i=4,6} \Psi_{{\rm f}_i}(\mathbf{C}, \mathbf{H}_i) $$

The contributions are specified as:

$$ \Psi_{\text{iso}} = \frac{\mu}{2} \left( {I}_1 - 3 \right) $$ $$ \sum_{i=4,6} \Psi_{{\rm f}_i} = \frac{k_1}{2 k_2} \left( \exp[ k_2 E_4^2] - 1 \right) + \frac{k_1}{2 k_2} \left( \exp[ k_2 E_6^2] - 1 \right) $$

The fiber dispersion is modeled by out-of-plane ($\kappa_{\text{op}}$) and in-plane ($\kappa_{\text{ip}}$) parameters which define structural tensors $\mathbf{H}_i$. The fiber families are oriented at angles $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$.
